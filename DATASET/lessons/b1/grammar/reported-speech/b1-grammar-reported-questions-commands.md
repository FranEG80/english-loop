# Resumen

Las preguntas en estilo indirecto usan orden afirmativo, no orden interrogativo:

`“Where is the station?”`  
`She asked where the station was.`

Las preguntas de respuesta sí/no se introducen con `if` o `whether`:

`“Is the shop open?”`  
`He asked if the shop was open.`

Las órdenes e instrucciones se transforman con `tell + persona + to +
infinitivo`. Las peticiones suelen usar `ask + persona + to + infinitivo`:

`“Close the door.”` → She told me to close the door.  
`“Please help me.”` → She asked me to help her.

Las formas negativas usan `not to`: `He told us not to wait`.

# Objetivos

- Construir `asked + persona + if/whether + sujeto + verbo`.
- Mantener la partícula interrogativa en preguntas con `what`, `where`, `when`,
  `why`, `who` y `how`.
- Eliminar `do`, `does` y `did` al pasar a orden afirmativo.
- Aplicar el backshift y los cambios de referencia necesarios.
- Usar `told + persona + to + infinitivo` para órdenes e instrucciones.
- Usar `asked + persona + to + infinitivo` para peticiones.
- Formar órdenes y peticiones negativas con `not to`.
- Distinguir `asked me if ...` de `asked me to ...`.

# Explicación

## Preguntas de sí/no

No tienen una partícula interrogativa propia. Añadimos `if` o `whether`:

`“Do you need a receipt?”`  
`The cashier asked me if I needed a receipt.`

`“Has the bus left?”`  
`Nora asked whether the bus had left.`

Después de `if/whether` usamos orden afirmativo:

`if I needed`, no `if did I need`  
`whether the bus had left`, no `whether had the bus left`

## Preguntas con wh-

Conservamos la partícula:

`“Where do you live?”`  
`He asked me where I lived.`

`“Why is the gate locked?”`  
`She asked why the gate was locked.`

`“When did the meeting start?”`  
`They asked when the meeting had started.`

El verbo introductor `asked` convierte toda la estructura en una oración
subordinada; por eso desaparecen la inversión y los auxiliares `do/does/did`.

## Órdenes e instrucciones

Usamos:

`told + persona + to + infinitivo`

`“Keep the receipt.”`  
`The assistant told me to keep the receipt.`

`“Turn left at the lights.”`  
`The guide told us to turn left at the lights.`

`Tell` necesita la persona que recibe la instrucción. No decimos `told to keep`
sin indicar a quién.

## Peticiones

Para una petición cortés usamos normalmente:

`asked + persona + to + infinitivo`

`“Please send me the invoice.”`  
`Rina asked Omar to send her the invoice.`

`Ask` refleja que la persona solicita la acción, no que formula una pregunta
sobre información.

## Formas negativas

La negación se coloca antes del infinitivo:

`“Don't touch the screen.”`  
`The technician told us not to touch the screen.`

`“Please don't mention my address.”`  
`Maya asked me not to mention her address.`

No usamos `to not` como patrón editorial principal en B1.

# Forma o estructura

## Pregunta sí/no

- `asked + persona + if/whether + sujeto + verbo`

`He asked me if I was ready.`  
`She asked whether the payment had arrived.`

## Pregunta con wh-

- `asked + persona + wh-word + sujeto + verbo`

`They asked us where we were staying.`  
`I asked her what she wanted.`

## Orden

- `told + persona + to + infinitivo`
- `told + persona + not to + infinitivo`

`She told him to wait outside.`  
`She told him not to block the entrance.`

## Petición

- `asked + persona + to + infinitivo`
- `asked + persona + not to + infinitivo`

`He asked me to speak more slowly.`  
`He asked me not to share the code.`

# Backshift y referencias

Se aplican los mismos cambios que en los enunciados:

`“Are you working today?”`  
Al informar después: `She asked me if I was working that day.`

`“Where did you put these boxes?”`  
`He asked where I had put those boxes.`

`“Will you be here tomorrow?”`  
`They asked whether I would be there the next day.`

En órdenes y peticiones, el verbo principal se convierte en infinitivo y no
necesita un tiempo pasado:

`“Wait here.”` → He told me to wait there.

# Usos principales

1. **Pregunta cerrada:** `She asked if the room was available.`
2. **Elección formal:** `He asked whether I wanted tea or coffee.`
3. **Lugar:** `They asked where we had parked.`
4. **Motivo:** `The manager asked why the order was late.`
5. **Cantidad:** `She asked how much the repair would cost.`
6. **Instrucción:** `The guard told us to use the side entrance.`
7. **Prohibición:** `The guide told visitors not to feed the animals.`
8. **Petición:** `Marta asked me to check the address.`
9. **Petición negativa:** `Leo asked us not to call before nine.`
10. **Referencia cambiada:** `He asked whether I could meet him there the next
    day.`

# Contrastes importantes

## Asked if frente a asked to

`She asked me if I could help.`  
Preguntó si yo tenía capacidad o disponibilidad.

`She asked me to help.`  
Me pidió que ayudara.

La primera estructura contiene una pregunta; la segunda solicita una acción.

## Tell frente a ask

`The supervisor told me to restart the machine.`  
Instrucción directa.

`The supervisor asked me to restart the machine.`  
Petición, normalmente menos autoritaria.

La intención del hablante y el contexto determinan la elección.

## Orden directo frente a orden afirmativo

Directa: `Where is the office?`  
Indirecta: `She asked where the office was.`

No: `She asked where was the office.`

Directa: `What did you choose?`  
Indirecta: `He asked what I had chosen.`

No conservamos `did`.

## Who como sujeto

Si `who` ya es el sujeto de la pregunta directa, no hay inversión que eliminar:

`“Who called you?”`  
`She asked who had called me.`

No añadimos otro sujeto: no `who someone had called`.

# Ejemplos

1. **The receptionist asked if I had an appointment.**
2. **Mina asked whether the lift was working.**
3. **The driver asked us where we wanted to get off.**
4. **I asked Leo why he had changed the route.**
5. **They asked how long the repair would take.**
6. **The nurse told me to sit near the window.**
7. **The sign told visitors not to enter the construction area.**
8. **Ravi asked me to forward the confirmation email.**
9. **Nora asked us not to leave bags in the corridor.**
10. **The organiser asked whether we could return there the following day.**

# Errores frecuentes

- ❌ *She asked where was the station.*  
  ✅ *She asked where the station was.*  
  La pregunta reportada usa orden afirmativo.

- ❌ *He asked me where did I park.*  
  ✅ *He asked me where I had parked.*  
  Eliminamos `did` y hacemos el backshift.

- ❌ *They asked that the shop was open.*  
  ✅ *They asked if the shop was open.*  
  Una pregunta sí/no usa `if` o `whether`.

- ❌ *The guard told to wait outside.*  
  ✅ *The guard told us to wait outside.*  
  `Tell` necesita la persona.

- ❌ *Maya asked me that help her.*  
  ✅ *Maya asked me to help her.*  
  La petición usa objeto + infinitivo.

- ❌ *He told me don't touch the switch.*  
  ✅ *He told me not to touch the switch.*  
  La orden negativa usa `not to`.

- ❌ *She asked me if to close the door.*  
  ✅ *She asked me to close the door.*  
  Se solicita una acción, no una respuesta sí/no.

# Excepciones relevantes

`Whether` es especialmente útil antes de `or not` y en elecciones:

`She asked whether I wanted to stay or leave.`  
`He asked whether or not the fee was refundable.`

En conversación, `if` es muy frecuente en preguntas cerradas ordinarias.

Una petición formulada con `Could you ...?` o `Would you ...?` se transforma
normalmente con `asked + persona + to`, no como pregunta sobre capacidad:

`“Could you open the window, please?”`  
`She asked me to open the window.`

Sin embargo, `“Can you swim?”` sí pregunta por capacidad:

`She asked me if I could swim.`

La intención comunicativa decide la estructura.

Las preguntas reportadas integradas en una oración suelen terminar con punto,
no con signo de interrogación, porque toda la frase es declarativa: `She asked
where I lived.`

# Mini resumen

- Sí/no → `asked if/whether + orden afirmativo`.
- Wh-question → `asked + wh-word + orden afirmativo`.
- Elimina `do/does/did`.
- Aplica backshift y cambios de referencia.
- Orden → `told + persona + to`.
- Petición → `asked + persona + to`.
- Negativa → `not to`.
- `asked if` pregunta información; `asked to` solicita una acción.

# Comprobación rápida autocorregible

1. “Do you need help?” she asked me.  
   She asked me ___ I needed help.
   - Respuesta: `if`
   - Explicación: es una pregunta sí/no.
2. “Where is the exit?” he asked.  
   He asked where the exit ___.
   - Respuesta: `was`
   - Explicación: orden afirmativo y backshift de `is`.
3. “Keep this ticket,” the inspector told us.  
   The inspector told us ___ keep that ticket.
   - Respuesta: `to`
   - Explicación: una instrucción usa `told + objeto + to`.
4. “Please send me the file,” Ana said to Leo.  
   Ana asked Leo ___ send her the file.
   - Respuesta: `to`
   - Explicación: es una petición.
5. “Don't open the package,” the courier told me.  
   The courier told me ___ open the package.
   - Respuesta: `not to`
   - Explicación: la orden negativa usa `not to`.
