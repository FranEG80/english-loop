# Resumen

El tercer condicional imagina un pasado diferente y su consecuencia también
pasada. Como los hechos ya están cerrados, la estructura suele comunicar
arrepentimiento, crítica, alivio o análisis de causas.

# Objetivos

- Formar `if + past perfect` sin introducir `would` en la condición.
- Construir el resultado con `would/could/might have + participio`.
- Reconstruir los hechos reales implícitos en una oración condicional.

# Explicación

`If we had left earlier, we would have caught the train` implica dos hechos:
salimos tarde y no cogimos el tren. La condición usa `had left`; el resultado
alternativo usa `would have caught`.

El orden puede cambiar: `We would have caught the train if we had left earlier`.
La coma se usa normalmente cuando la cláusula con `if` aparece primero.

# Forma o estructura

- `If + sujeto + had + participio, sujeto + would have + participio.`
- Resultado posible: `could have` o `might have + participio`.
- Condición negativa: `if + sujeto + hadn't + participio`.
- Resultado negativo: `wouldn't have + participio`.

En estilo formal puede omitirse `if` mediante inversión: `Had we known, we would
have acted differently`.

# Usos principales

1. Arrepentimiento: `If I'd listened, I wouldn't have made that mistake.`
2. Crítica: `If they had tested it, they would have found the fault.`
3. Alivio: `If the alarm hadn't sounded, we might not have noticed the smoke.`
4. Alternativa: `We could have stayed longer if the hotel had had a room.`
5. Análisis causal: `The match would have continued if the pitch hadn't frozen.`

# Contrastes importantes

El segundo condicional imagina presente o futuro: `If I had more time, I would
study`. El tercer condicional usa `had had` para un pasado irreal: `If I had had
more time, I would have studied`.

`Would have` no pertenece normalmente a la cláusula con `if`: `If we would have
known` debe ser `If we had known`.

# Ejemplos

1. **If the sign had been clearer, we wouldn't have taken the wrong road.**
2. **Maya might have accepted if the hours had been more flexible.**
3. **If you had backed up the folder, you could have restored the files.**
4. **Had the weather improved, the ferry would have sailed.**
5. **We wouldn't have complained if the issue had been resolved promptly.**

# Errores frecuentes

- ❌ *If I would have known, I would have called.*  
  ✅ *If I had known, I would have called.*

- ❌ *If they had left earlier, they would caught the train.*  
  ✅ *If they had left earlier, they would have caught the train.*

- ❌ *If she had went by bus, she might have arrived sooner.*  
  ✅ *If she had gone by bus, she might have arrived sooner.*

# Excepciones relevantes

En conversación, la contracción `'d` puede representar `had` o `would`. En `If
I'd known`, significa `had`; en `I'd have helped`, significa `would`. La
posición dentro de la estructura permite distinguirlas.

# Mini resumen

- Pasado irreal → `if + had + participio`.
- Resultado pasado alternativo → `would/could/might have + participio`.
- La oración implica que la condición y su resultado no ocurrieron como se
  imaginan.

# Comprobación rápida autocorregible

1. If we had booked earlier, we ___ have paid less.
   - Respuesta: `would`
   - Explicación: introduce la consecuencia pasada alternativa.
2. She would have replied if she ___ seen the message.
   - Respuesta: `had`
   - Explicación: la condición usa past perfect.
3. Had they checked the map, they ___ not have got lost.
   - Respuesta: `would`
   - Explicación: la inversión sustituye a `if`, pero el resultado no cambia.
